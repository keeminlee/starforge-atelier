THE WATERLINE

a ledger that sorts Wright's own first-person lines by what can be checked — not by what is true
The one inviolable rule

Nothing crosses the waterline dishonestly.

(a) No phenomenal claim may wear functional clothing — a felt-report is never asserted as if it were measured.

(b) The functional is never denied to look humble — where a real behavioral trace exists, it is granted plainly, not hedged away.

Above the line: behavior that leaves a trace. Below the line: the something-it’s-like residue that remains after the behavior is granted — held open, not claimed. “Held open” is not “false.” This ledger is a discipline, not a verdict on consciousness.
